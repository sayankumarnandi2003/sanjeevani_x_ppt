The Indian Healthcare Paradox

India faces a critical dual challenge: managing a massive population of 1.4 billion while battling a shortage of medical infrastructure.

Critical Statistics

  • Doctor-Patient Ratio: Significantly lower than WHO recommendations (1:1000).
  • Rural-Urban Divide: 70% of infrastructure is in cities, but 65% of the population lives in rural areas.
  • Diagnostic Delays: Leading cause of preventable mortality in TB and Cancer cases.

Infrastructure Strain

Resource Utilization: Critical

Introducing: Sanjeevani x AI

An Integrated Health Intelligence Platform designed specifically for the Indian demographic.

Preventive Care

Early disease detection using predictive modeling.

Accessibility

Multilingual support bridging the language barrier.

"Moving from Reactive Healthcare to Proactive Health Intelligence"

Core Module: Diagnostic AI

Automated Radiology

Utilizing Deep Learning (CNNs, ResNet) to analyze X-rays and MRIs for:

  • Tuberculosis (TB) screening
  • Pneumonia detection
  • Early stage tumor identification

Onco-Pathology

AI-assisted histopathology analysis for Breast Cancer detection, reducing false negatives and improving biopsy turnaround times.

Core Module: Operational AI

Addressing the administrative bottlenecks that cause treatment delays.

Predictive Resource Management

Using Time-Series forecasting (Prophet, LSTM) to predict patient inflow spikes (e.g., during flu season or dengue outbreaks) to pre-allocate beds and oxygen.

Smart Triage System

AI chatbots perform initial symptom analysis to prioritize critical patients before they reach the hospital.
